Photovoltaic glossary

Different parameters are taken into account to define the optimal sizing of your solar installation: roof surface, roof inclination, installation environment, panel performance, etc. Sud Concept supports you in defining the installation that will maximize your solar performance.
Photons, which are particles that make up solar radiation, transmit their energy to electrons when they encounter the surface of a photovoltaic cell. This creates a direct electric current which will be transformed into alternating current by the inverter in order to be fed back into the distribution network.
Solar electricity is electricity produced from solar energy through photovoltaic modules.
Renewable energies mainly include solar, wind, hydraulic, biomass and geothermal energies. It is nature, by the sun, the wind, the waves, the tides, etc. which is at the origin of these energies.
Solar energy is energy that comes from radiation (direct or diffuse) from the sun. On our planet, solar energy is at the origin of the water and wind cycle. Plants use it by transforming it into chemical energy through photosynthesis. It is through different processes that solar energy can be transformed into another energy: electricity thanks to photovoltaic solar energy, heat thanks to solar thermal energy ...
The solar panels are mounted directly on a frame, in place of the roof (tiles, steel pan, zinc, etc.) according to an installation procedure that guarantees perfect waterproofing of your roof. Sud Concept strongly advises you to opt for this solution which promotes the aesthetics of buildings and allows you to benefit from the solar electricity buy-back rate at the most attractive price: € 0.388 / kWh (2012 rate).
The kWh (kilo Watt hour) is the traditional unit of measurement for electrical energy. This corresponds to the operation of a power of 1 kW for 1 hour. 1000 kWh corresponds to 1 MWh (Mega Watt hour).
A photovoltaic module is used to generate electricity from sunlight. It is a unit made up of several photovoltaic cells electrically connected and ready for installation. Several modules constitute a photovoltaic generator.
Monocrystalline solar panels are the panels with the highest rates of return. Their manufacturing cycle is very complex, so they are relatively expensive compared to their productivity gain. The silicon atoms in monocrystalline photovoltaic cells are ordered in a regular fashion, like the carbon atoms of a diamond.
Photovoltaic solar panels silently produce clean energy without any CO2 emissions. Sud Concept offers you modules composed of photovoltaic cells allowing the transformation of solar radiation into electricity. These cells are essentially composed of silicon, a semiconductor material resulting from the transformation of silica (sand), an inexhaustible and abundant constituent (28% of the earth's crust). Mainly mounted on the roofs and on the terraces of buildings, photovoltaic solar panels can also be mounted on the facade or on the ground for high power systems. When photons (particles that make up solar radiation) meet the surface of a photovoltaic cell, they transmit their energy to the electrons in the cell. As they move, these electrons create a direct electric current which is then transformed by an inverter into alternating current in order to be fed back into the distribution network.
The solar thermal panel does not produce electricity but collects energy from the sun to produce hot water for your heating system and for your hot water tank.
Photovoltaics is the effect by which light energy is transformed into electrical energy or solar electricity.

How do clouds affect the performance of a solar panel?

The sun does not have to be visible for a photovoltaic solar panel to work. Diffuse radiation from clouds is also used to generate solar electricity. The efficiency of the panel is proportional to the perceived external luminosity.

What will be the annual yield of my solar photovoltaic installation?

The productivity of a solar photovoltaic installation depends on several factors: the orientation of the roof or the solar installation the inclination of the installation the presence or absence of cast shadows local radiation values ​​weather conditions. Depending on the region, a photovoltaic solar installation in France produces 700 to 1300 kWh per year for each kWp of installed power.

What is an inverter?

An inverter is used to standardize the solar electricity produced. It transforms the direct current produced by photovoltaic solar modules into alternating current which can then be fed back into the electricity distribution network.

Should the solar photovoltaic installation be cleaned regularly?

The rain will simply take care of the cleaning necessary for the proper functioning of the solar photovoltaic installation. However, there are regions subject to high atmospheric pollution (industrial complexes), where cleaning of the solar collectors at long intervals may be necessary.

Is a photovoltaic solar installation in the North interesting?

Whatever region of France you live in, it is interesting to have a solar installation. The same installation will produce only a little less solar energy in the North of France than in the South because the annual production depends on the sunshine. Germany, for example, and its 200,000 individual customer installations confirms the interest of photovoltaics for areas with less sunlight.

How do you know if a house is suitable for mounting a solar installation?

In general, almost all buildings are suitable for mounting a solar installation. The photovoltaic solar installation requires a mounting surface on the roof or on the facade, facing south, east or west. A south-facing mounting surface with a 30 ° tilt provides optimum performance. A shift towards the South-East / South-West, or an inclination between 25 ° and 60 °, only slightly decreases the energy efficiency of the light. However, surfaces that receive shadows cast by trees, neighboring houses or a fireplace (which can also be called cast shadow) should be avoided.

How is the current injected into the public network?

The current created by your solar installation is fed into the public grid by installing an additional meter. If the installation still has a free space, this can be used for the new meter, after having agreed with the electricity supplier. In other cases, an additional small meter cabinet can be fitted.

How to install photovoltaic solar panels on a building?

Two types of installation are possible:
superimposed: the photovoltaic solar modules are simply attached to the existing roof in integrated integration into the building: the photovoltaic solar modules participate in the structure of the building (integrated in the roof, sun shading, facade, glass roof, railing, spandrel, etc.). Purchase prices are specific to each type of installation.

How and to whom to invoice its solar production?

On each anniversary date of the commissioning of your installation, you must invoice EDF for the electricity produced by your photovoltaic installation during the past year, to benefit from your solar income.

What are the main types of photovoltaic solar panels?

Monocrystalline solar panels are the panels with the highest rates of return. Their manufacturing cycle is very complex, so they are relatively expensive compared to their productivity gain. Amorphous (or micro-amorphous) solar panels have lower rates of return but are significantly more active in low light (cloudy weather, shade). They cost less, but produce significantly less energy than crystalline panels.
Polycrystalline (or multicrystalline) solar panels: provide the most attractive cost / productivity ratio. This is the reason why a large majority of installations currently use this type of panel.

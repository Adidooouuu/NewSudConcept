What is MaPrimeRénov '?

MaPrimeRénov ’is state aid for households wishing to carry out energy renovation work on their homes.

Who can benefit ?

Since the recovery plan announced on September 3, 2020, MaPrimeRénov ’has become accessible to all owner-occupants, modest or not. The only conditions to access it:

  • Being the owner of a home built for more than 2 years
  • The work must be carried out by a company Recognized as Guarantor of the Environment (RGE)

Since January 1, 2021, all previously ineligible households and condominiums can also submit their application. If you are part of a co-ownership, only one collective aid will be paid to the union without taking into account the individual situations of the occupants or lessors. If you are a landlord, you can also benefit from MaPrimeRénov, to encourage the energy renovation of housing in the private rental stock. However, the filing of files will not open until July 1, 2021.

How is the aid calculated?

This aid is calculated according to three criteria:

  • Household income
  • The energy savings made possible by this work
  • The heat production allowed by the devices put in place

Its amount, known upstream of the works, is paid following their completion. To find out more, visit the MaPrimeRénov’.fr website.

The ANAH

ANAH is a public institution placed under the supervision of the ministries in charge of territorial cohesion and relations with local authorities, of Public Action and Accounts and of the Ministry of the Economy and Finance.
Its mission, for nearly 50 years, has been to improve the condition of the private housing stock, thereby reducing social and territorial divides.
The ANAH is also fighting against fuel poverty through its "Habiter Mieux" program.
Its usefulness is to ensure that the work guaranteeing a real energy gain can be done. In addition to support, ANAH offers two different financial offers:
  • Habiter Mieux
  • Habiter Mieux sérénité
Previously, there was a third offer, "Habiter Mieux agilité", which no longer exists and is now merged with the EWCs to give MaPrimeRénov ". These aids cannot be combined with MaPrimeRénov ’, however they can be combined with the energy voucher and reduced rate VAT.
The following categories are affected:
  • Community
  • Owner occupants
  • Landlord owners
  • Condominiums
  • Professionnals
It all depends on your category. To find out more, visit anah.fr.

Depending on the work carried out in your main or secondary residence, completed more than two years ago, you can benefit from reduced VAT rates instead of the normal rate of 20%.

The 10% VAT rate provided for in article 279-0 bis of the General Tax Code (CGI) applies to improvement, transformation, development and maintenance work, other than construction work. or reconstruction and expansion (subject to the standard rate of 20%), and excluding energy quality improvement work which falls under the 5.5% VAT rate provided for in article 278-0 bis A of the CGI.

Important : the rates indicated above are those in force in mainland France since January 1, 2014. Specific rates exist in the overseas departments.

This work is subject to the standard rate of VAT of 20% provided for in article 278 of the CGI. Reduced VAT rates do not apply to work which:
  • relate to premises other than living quarters;
  • relate to residential premises completed less than two years ago;
  • lead to an elevation of the building or an addition of construction;
  • increase the floor area of ​​the existing construction by more than 10%;
  • contribute to the production of a new building.
The building is considered new for tax purposes in one of the following cases:
  • if the work returns more than half ofthe three structural elements to new condition, i.e. if the work relates either to the majority of the foundations (> 50%), or to the majority of elements excluding foundations which determine the strength and rigidity of the building (frameworks, load-bearing walls> 50%), ie on the majority of the consistency of the facades, excluding renovation (> 50%);
  • if the works restore at least two-thirds (2/3) of each of the six finishing elements which are the non-load-bearing floors, the exterior frames, the interior partitions, the sanitary and plumbing installations, electrical installations and heating systems in mainland France only.
Work that returns a building to new condition as well as work that increases the floor area of ​​the existing construction by more than 10% is assessed over a period of two years preceding or following completion of the work. Examples of works and associated VAT rates :

These works which return the building to new condition are subject to the normal rate of 20% except in Corsica where real estate works are subject to a special rate of 10%. The standard 20% VAT rate also applies:
  • cleaning work,
  • landscaping and maintenance work on green spaces,
  • the supply of household or furniture equipment,
  • the acquisition of certain large equipment such as :
- heating system: the following collective equipment located in a building with several rooms; boiler used as a heating or hot water production method, oil tank, gas tank and heat pump; - elevator ; - sanitary facilities: hammam or sauna cabin ready to install; - all air conditioning systems, as well as air / air type heat pumps.
The premises must be completed for more than 2 years, on the date of the start of the work. The premises must be allocated to housing, whether it is your main or secondary residence. These are individual houses, apartments and their usual outbuildings (cellars, attics, terraces, courtyards of buildings, etc.). In apartment buildings, both private and common areas are concerned.
Materials and equipment that you buy yourself for installation by a company are subject to the standard rate of 20%. In this case, only the installation service benefits from the reduced rate. To benefit from reduced VAT rates, the work must meet the following cumulative conditions :
  • relate to premises for residential use completed more than 2 years ago ;
  • not to contribute over a period of 2 years to the production of a new building for tax purposes ;
  • do not increase the floor area of ​​existing premises by more than 10% over a period of 2 years.
Reminder : the work is assessed over a period of 2 years.
To determine at the end of the work the proportion of structural and / or finishing elements returned to new condition, you must take into account the work already carried out in the previous two years. Likewise, the benefit of reduced VAT rates is likely to be called into question by any work that would take place in the next two years.

Energy quality improvement work
This concerns the supply and installation of energy-saving materials and equipment. Eligible works are those mentioned in 1 of article 200 quater of the CGI, provided that they meet the minimum performance criteria detailed in article 18 bis of appendix 4 of the CGI.
  • Expenses for the following energy savings:
  • acquisition of gas micro-cogeneration boilers with an electrical production power less than or equal to 3 kilovolt-amperes per dwelling;
  • acquisition of condensing boilers;
  • acquisition of heating control devices.
  • Thermal insulation expenses for opaque walls (walls facing the outside, roofs, attic ceilings);
  • Thermal insulation expenses for glass walls (windows, patio doors, roof windows), insulating shutters or entrance doors to the outside;
  • Energy production equipment using a renewable energy source (wood boilers, wood or pellet stoves, heat pumps other than air / air, heating or domestic hot water supply equipment operating on energy solar or hydraulic ...)
Please note, photovoltaic panels are not affected by the 5.5% rate. The complete list of eligible equipment, materials or appliances as well as the performance criteria applicable to them can be viewed on this site under the Individuals section “My home, my heritage” “The energy transition tax credit (CITE)” “Expenses eligible for CITE ”
Note : To benefit from the 5.5% rate, it is not necessary to be eligible for the energy transition tax credit (CITE) but the materials and equipment installed by companies must meet all the technical characteristics and criteria. of energy performance retained for this tax credit.
Work induced by energy improvement work subject to 5.5%
The induced work is the ancillary work made essential by the laying or installation work of eligible materials and equipment at the rate of 5.5%.
They do not apply to aesthetic work or other renovation work carried out concurrently. Induced work must be invoiced within a maximum period of three months from the date of invoicing for the energy improvement work to which they are related. When the induced work precedes the energy improvement work to which they are linked (drilling and earthworks necessary for the installation of the underground heat exchanger for geothermal heat pumps), the energy improvement work must be invoiced. within a maximum period of three months from the date of invoicing for the induced work. If this deadline is not respected, the induced work is then assessed as independent work which should not be invoiced at the rate of 5.5%.
Only works and equipment invoiced directly to the customer by a company are affected by the reduced VAT rates. Materials and equipment that you buy yourself for installation by a company are subject to the standard rate of 20%. In this case, only the installation service benefits from the reduced rate. As a customer, you can own, rent or occupy free of charge.
To benefit from reduced VAT rates, you must give each professional, before invoicing, a certificate confirming compliance with the conditions for applying these rates.
Please note : the work is assessed over a period of 2 years. There are 2 models of certificate with notice at your disposal :
  • the normal certificate N ° 1300-SD is to be used for work affecting the shell or the six elements of the shell.
  • the simplified certificate No. 1301-SD must be used for all work which does not affect any of the structural elements and not more than five of the six structural elements.
This certificate is essential for professionals to be able to invoice the work at reduced rates. You must keep the copy of the certificate and all invoices issued by the service providers until December 31 of the 5th year following completion of the work. They must be produced if the administration asks you to justify the application of reduced VAT rates.

These certificates must be produced each time a work provider intervenes. However, in order to reduce the administrative burden weighing on customers and professionals, it is accepted that the certificate n ° 1301-SD is not established when the amount of work for repair and maintenance, all taxes included, is less than 300 euros. In this case, the following information must appear on the invoice: name and address of the customer and of the building subject to the work, nature of the work and mention that the building has been completed for more than 2 years.

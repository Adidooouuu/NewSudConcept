Sud Concept offers 100% solar hybrid solutions, that is to say solutions combining photovoltaics and solar heating. This hybrid solution therefore produces both electricity and heat.

Benefits :

  • Compactness and discretion: the heat recovery system is integrated into the solar panels so it does not increase the surface area occupied by the installation.
  • Increase in efficiency: on a conventional photovoltaic installation, the efficiency of the panels decreases as their temperature increases. For a hybrid installation, the temperature of the panels is regulated because the solar heating system draws its energy from the heat of the panels. On average and depending on climatic conditions, the increase in photovoltaic efficiency is estimated at 20%
  • Silence: an all-solar hybrid solution produces no noise.
Both very economical and ecological, a solar water heater allows you to greatly limit the use of your conventional boiler (gas, fuel oil) or your electric water heater.

The principle

A solar water heater is made up of 2 main elements:
  • solar collectors
  • the storage tank
The collectors are installed outside and capture the heat from solar radiation in order to heat the water. Be careful, these solar collectors have nothing to do with the photovoltaic modules which give them electricity at the output.
The ball is installed inside the house. It is used to store and provide you with water heated by the sensors during the day. The link between sensors and balloon is made by circulation in a hydraulic system.
Remember that a solar water heater is not necessarily completely autonomous. Depending on the region, your needs and the average sunshine, a conventional water heater may be necessary to compensate for the lack of sun on certain days.

Presentation

Sud Concept develops and adapts autonomous solar / wind kits for isolated sites according to your needs. Developing clean, silent and emission-free energy, our solar kits based on photovoltaic panels and / or wind turbines are able to cover the electricity needs of many sites not connected to the grid. In addition, our equipment is designed to operate in all climates.
Based on your energy needs, the climate and the geographical location of the site, we adapt an autonomous kit which will be based on photovoltaic panels and / or a wind turbine.
Our autonomous solar / wind kits are made up of:
  • One or more photovoltaic panels and / or one or more wind turbines. The powers of these elements are chosen according to your needs and the place of installation,
  • A charge regulator,
  • An inverter to deliver an alternating voltage of 220V or 380V to the site,
  • One or more batteries that recharge during the day and supply your equipment at night, in the event of lack of sun, lack of wind or in the event of consumption peaks on your site,
  • Supports for photovoltaic panels

Examples of use

To bring you an autonomous solar kit offering the best performance, it is preferable to have your solutions precisely sized by our team. However, here are some typical examples:
Autonomous solar kit 200W
A 200W solar kit will most often consist of 2 photovoltaic panels of 100W each. Depending on the climate and the amount of sunlight, the solution can produce around 1.1kWh per day. You can thus supply:
  • 4 compact fluorescent bulbs 13W, 5h per day: 260Wh
  • A laptop (50W), 6h per day: 300Wh
  • A class A + refrigerator over one day: 550Wh
Autonomous solar kit 1000W
A 1000W solar kit can produce up to 5.5 kWh per day. With this kit you can autonomously supply an isolated site with the following equipment:
  • 6 compact fluorescent bulbs 13W, 5h per day: 390Wh
  • A large capacity refrigerator over a day: 1000Wh
  • 2 laptops (50W), 8h per day: 800W
  • A 2kW kitchen oven, 30 min per day: 1000Wh
  • A submerged pump

Hydroelectric power, or hydroelectricity, is electrical energy obtained by transforming the energy (kinetic or gravity) of water in a stream or body of water. The force of the water is converted into electrical energy using a hydroturbine and then an alternator. This is the principle used in hydroelectric dams operated by EDF, for example. The amount of energy produced will mainly depend on the flow of water present.
This source of energy has an undeniable advantage: production is continuous and regular with very low sensitivity to weather conditions. Unlike a photovoltaic or wind power plant, production does not stop at night or in the absence of wind.
Completely clean, this energy can be stored in electrical form in a battery kit to cushion a drop in production or overconsumption on the site.

Solar lighting
A solar street light stores energy during the day, the cells of its photovoltaic panel recharge the batteries.
At night, at a chosen time, the lamppost comes on, using only the energy contained in its batteries.
We adapt the battery capacity according to your lighting needs and average sunlight of the location where the lighting solution is installed.
